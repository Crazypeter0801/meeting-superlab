# Competitor Research Dimensions

Use these dimensions for meeting, collaboration, AI meeting, chat, community, and async communication products.

## Product Sets

- Meeting/video: Zoom, Microsoft Teams, Google Meet, Webex, Tencent Meeting.
- Enterprise collaboration: Feishu/Lark, DingTalk, Slack.
- Community/chat/voice: Discord, Telegram.
- AI meeting workflow references: Fireflies, Otter.ai, Read AI, Granola.

## Dimensions

1. Entry and trigger: where the feature starts, what prompts the user, what is default.
2. Core flow: steps, interruptions, confirmations, shortcuts, and completion criteria.
3. Information structure: navigation, panels, cards, timeline, lists, detail pages, search.
4. Role and permission model: host, participant, guest, admin, owner, external user, channel member.
5. Collaboration and handoff: comments, mentions, sharing, tasks, reminders, notifications.
6. AI assistance: summarization, action items, recommendations, retrieval, automation, explainability.
7. Trust and control: editability, source trace, confidence, consent, privacy cues, undo.
8. States: normal, empty, loading, error, permission denied, no data, external guest, mobile/desktop.
9. Visual density: whether the pattern is compact, panel-based, full-screen, card-heavy, list-first, or chat-first.
10. Differentiation: what Tencent Meeting should borrow, adapt, avoid, or uniquely own.

## Evidence Labels

- Fact: directly observed from source, screenshot, or product use.
- Interpretation: likely meaning or design rationale.
- Hypothesis: plausible but unverified behavior.
- Opportunity: product/design implication for Tencent Meeting.
